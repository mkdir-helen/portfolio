About Me

I used to think science was my thing when I studied Biology in college. It wasn't really. I didn't hate it--but I never felt enough passion to mingle in a party of science lovers or push myself to take a new learning challenge. After several years of wandering and getting lost in the search of a career path, I decided to switch to a different field--Professional and Technical Writing. The switch was worth it. I really enjoyed learning about the specific design principles and grammar rules that create a better user experience. It intrigued me to think that one slight change of a page element can instantly transform a layout into an ugly or beautiful art. But I still felt I needed something more. While working within the designing framework, I felt my creativity was limited to the rules often set by opinions of professionals or users; or sometimes I felt did not have enough creativity with the few resource ideas to invent an innovative, enhanced user experience (since basic rules of designs and grammar have remained unchanged for decades).

        One day, while I was browsing through tutorials on Photoshop, I came across courses on how to code websites. I decided to give learning websites a try and I immediately fell in love with the world of coding. It was more than just building blocks of rules but continually conducting electricity of ideas that flows as long as a skeleton of connection is being built. The reason why I love coding language is, it gives me this kind of satisfaction similar to when you finish fitting the puzzle pieces together, except it's different in that there is an unlimited supply of puzzle pieces; and it's up to your creative instinct as how you organize or pick the right pieces--whether it's trending or fits "just right."
